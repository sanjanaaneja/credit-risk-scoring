# Credit Risk Scoring: Executive Summary

## Situation

A European bank processes over 5,000 SME loan applications annually across 7 countries and 10 industry sectors. Total portfolio exposure stands at approximately EUR 666M.

## Complication

The portfolio carries a 4.6% default rate, with high-risk concentration in Hospitality and Agriculture. Manual underwriting is slow and inconsistent, and there is no systematic way to differentiate pricing or approval speed by risk level.

## Key Findings

**1. Credit Bureau Score Is the Strongest Default Predictor**
Applicants with scores below 550 default at roughly double the rate of the broader portfolio. This is the clearest early warning signal available.

**2. Hospitality and Agriculture Carry Outsized Risk**
Agriculture defaults at 8.0% and Hospitality at 6.4%, nearly 2x the portfolio average of 4.6%. Healthcare is the safest sector at 2.8%.

**3. Existing Clients Default 24% Less Than New Applicants**
Existing bank clients default at 3.9% vs 5.1% for new applicants. Collateral further cuts default rates nearly in half across all loan sizes.

## Recommendations

| Action | Expected Impact | Priority |
|---|---|---|
| Risk-adjusted pricing by tier | +15 to 20 bps margin on high-risk loans | **High** |
| Tightened criteria for Hospitality and Agriculture | 25% reduction in sector default rate | **High** |
| Fast-track approvals for low-risk existing clients | 40% reduction in underwriting time | **Medium** |
